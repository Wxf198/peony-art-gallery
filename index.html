Our Story

The Art of Mudan

For centuries, the peony — known as Mudan (牡丹) in Chinese — has been celebrated as the "King of Flowers," symbolizing prosperity, honor, and beauty. From the imperial gardens of Chang'an to the galleries of the world, the peony's lush petals and rich cultural meaning have captivated art lovers across generations.

Our gallery is based in Luoyang, China — the historic peony capital — where master artists trained in the gongbi (fine brush) and xieyi (freehand) traditions bring these magnificent flowers to life on premium rice paper.

We believe in preserving this ancient craft while making it accessible to collectors around the world.

Craftsmanship

From Brush to Masterpiece

Every painting follows a time-honored process refined over generations.

01

Paper & Ink

We use Xuan paper (宣纸) — known as the "Paper of Ages" — handcrafted in Anhui province, and premium carbon ink sticks ground on ink stones for the deepest blacks.

02

Brush & Spirit

The artist enters a meditative state, channeling qi (气) through the brush. A single peony can require dozens of precise, unrepeatable strokes — each one capturing a moment of flow.

03

Seal & Signature

Each painting is finished with the artist's personal seal (印章) carved in red — a mark of authenticity and a connection to an unbroken lineage of masters.

04

Mounting & Delivery

Works are professionally mounted on archival materials, then carefully packed in custom crates for safe worldwide delivery.
